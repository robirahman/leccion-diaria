## 1. Grammar (C1 Additions)

### 1.1 Missing C1 Grammar Topics
- **Reported speech (Estilo indirecto avanzado)** — backshifting tenses, reporting questions, commands in indirect speech
- **Concessive clauses** — aunque + subjunctive vs indicative, por más que, si bien, aun cuando
- **Purpose & result clauses** — para que, a fin de que, de modo que (subjunctive vs indicative distinction)
- **Correlative constructions** — cuanto más…más, no solo…sino también, tanto…como
- **Advanced use of "se"** — se aspectual (se comió todo), se middle voice, impersonal se vs passive se disambiguation
- **Topicalization and left dislocation** — "A María, le di el libro" / "Lo que quiero es paz"
- **Redundant clitic constructions** — when and why le/lo doubling is required vs optional
- **Participle clauses** — absolute constructions ("Terminada la reunión, salimos")
- **Temporal clause subtleties** — antes de que (always subjunctive) vs después de que (indicative/subjunctive), en cuanto, apenas, no bien

### 1.2 Missing C1 Grammar Topics (Professional)
- **Formal letter/email conventions** — salutations, closings, formulaic expressions (Le saluda atentamente, Quedo a su disposición)
- **Presentation/argumentation discourse markers** — en primer lugar, cabe señalar, por consiguiente, en resumidas cuentas
- **Hedging and softening** — diría que, podría ser que, no es que…sino que

## 2. Grammar (C2 Additions)

### 2.1 Missing C2 Grammar Topics
- **Historical present and narrative tenses** — switching between preterite and present for literary effect
- **Subjunctive in fossilized expressions** — sea como sea, venga lo que venga, que yo sepa, o sea
- **Anacoluthon and oral syntax** — understanding natural speech breaks and restarts common in native oral Spanish
- **Impersonal infinitive constructions** — "El beber mucho daña la salud" vs "Beber mucho daña la salud"
- **Syntactic calques and false friends at the structural level** — "hacer una decisión" (Anglicism) vs "tomar una decisión"
- **Archaic and literary verb forms** — vosotros imperative in literary texts, future subjunctive in proverbs and legal texts
- **Advanced punctuation and orthographic conventions** — RAE 2010 rules, use of raya vs guion, comma before "y" in certain contexts
- **Dequeísmo and queísmo** — recognizing and avoiding these common native-speaker errors

### 2.2 Regional/Dialectal Grammar
- **Voseo conjugation patterns** — Argentine, Central American, and Colombian voseo differences
- **Ustedes vs vosotros** — Latin American universal ustedes, Canarian ustedes
- **Preterite vs present perfect regional variation** — Spain ("he comido") vs Latin America ("comí")
- **Diminutive regional patterns** — -ito/-ita (standard), -ico/-ica (Colombia/Costa Rica), -iño/-iña (Galician influence)

## 3. Reading Passages (C1/C2 Expansion)

### 3.1 C1 Reading Topics Needed
- **Opinion editorial** — immigration, technology, education policy (argumentative structure)
- **Scientific article abstract** — climate change, neuroscience, public health (academic register)
- **Legal notice / contract excerpt** — rental agreement, terms of service (legal register)
- **Literary criticism** — review of a novel or film with analytical vocabulary
- **Business report excerpt** — quarterly results, market analysis
- **Interview transcript** — with a public figure, mixing formal and informal register
- **Historical account** — a specific event narrated in journalistic style

### 3.2 C2 Reading Topics Needed
- **Academic paper introduction** — with citations, hypothesis, methodology description
- **Philosophical essay excerpt** — Ortega y Gasset, Octavio Paz, or similar
- **Legal text** — fragment of Spanish Constitution or civil code
- **Literary prose** — excerpt from García Márquez, Borges, Cortázar, or Rulfo
- **Satirical journalism** — irony, double meanings, cultural references
- **Dialectal text** — passage in rioplatense, Andean, or Caribbean Spanish
- **Poetry analysis** — interpreting metaphor, meter, and historical context
- **Transcribed debate** — with interruptions, hedging, and persuasive rhetoric

## 5. Cultural Content (C1/C2 Level Tags Needed)

### 5.1 Add CEFR Levels to Existing Modules
The following modules currently lack CEFR level tags. Adding levels would let them appear in level-appropriate study sessions:

- **Poetry** — tag by linguistic complexity (Neruda's simple love poems = B2, Góngora = C2)
- **Proverbs** — tag by vocabulary rarity and structural complexity
- **Idioms** — tag by opacity (transparent idioms = B2, opaque = C1/C2)
- **Jokes** — tag by cultural knowledge required and wordplay complexity
- **Festivals, History, Music, Movies, Sports, Travel** — tag by description complexity

### 5.2 New Cultural Content for C1/C2
- **Regional slang glossaries** — Mexican (chido, neta, mande), Argentine (boludo, re, posta), Colombian (bacano, parcero, chimba), Chilean (cachai, fome, po)

- **Historical linguistics** — Arabic influence on Spanish, indigenous language borrowings (nahuatl, quechua)

## 6. Practice Modules (C1/C2 Expansion)

### 6.1 Cloze Passages — New C1/C2 Topics
- C1: **Concessive clauses** (aunque + subj), **reported speech backshifting**, **advanced se constructions**, **formal email register**
- C2: **Literary pastiche** (fill blanks in style of García Márquez), **legal contract language**, **academic hedging** (cabe señalar, diríase que), **dialectal passages** (voseo, Andean)

### 6.2 Translation Drills — New C1/C2 Topics
- C1: **Untranslatable concepts** (sobremesa, madrugada, estrenar), **register shifting** (same message formal vs informal), **false friends in context**
- C2: **Poetry translation** (maintaining meter/rhyme), **legal clause translation**, **dialectal equivalents** (same meaning in Mexican vs Argentine Spanish), **humor/wordplay translation**

### 6.3 Dictation — New C1/C2 Topics
- C1: **News broadcast excerpts**, **podcast-style opinion pieces**, **formal speeches**
- C2: **Rapid natural speech with elision**, **dialectal audio** (Andalusian ceceo/seseo, Caribbean aspiration), **literary readings with archaic forms**, **simultaneous interpretation practice sentences**

### 6.4 New Practice Module: Error Correction (C1/C2)
A module where learners identify and fix errors in text — a skill tested in DELE C1/C2:
- Common native-speaker errors (dequeísmo, laísmo, haber/a ver)
- Anglicisms and false cognates in context
- Register mismatches (informal language in formal context)
- Punctuation and accent mark errors

### 6.5 New Practice Module: Paraphrase / Register Transformation (C1/C2)
Given a sentence, rewrite it in a different register:
- Informal → Formal ("Oye, ¿me pasas eso?" → "¿Sería tan amable de alcanzarme ese documento?")
- Active → Passive ("El gobierno aprobó la ley" → "La ley fue aprobada por el gobierno")
- Direct → Indirect speech
- Spoken → Written register

### 6.6 New Practice Module: Connectors in Context (C1/C2)
Expand the existing connectors module with C1/C2 exercises:
- **Concessive**: si bien, aun cuando, por más que, a pesar de que
- **Consecutive**: de ahí que, de modo que, así pues, por consiguiente
- **Reformulation**: es decir, o sea, dicho de otro modo, en otras palabras
- **Exemplification**: a saber, pongamos por caso, sin ir más lejos
- **Conclusion**: en resumidas cuentas, a fin de cuentas, en definitiva

## 8. "Beyond C2" — Near-Native and Heritage Speaker Content

For learners who have reached C2 and want to continue refining:

### 8.1 Dialectal Mastery
- **Andalusian Spanish** — aspiration of /s/, ceceo/seseo/distinción, loss of intervocalic /d/
- **Rioplatense** — sheísmo, voseo conjugation, Italian-influence intonation, lunfardo vocabulary
- **Caribbean** — /s/ aspiration/deletion, velarization of /n/, lambdacism/rotacism
- **Andean** — influence of Quechua (vowel merging, diminutive overuse, gerund constructions)
- **Mexican** — Nahuatl borrowings, güey/chido/neta, Mexican diminutive patterns
- **Chilean** — aspirated /s/, -po particle, weón, Chilean modismos

### 8.2 Stylistic Mastery
- **Registers across formality spectrum** — from vulgar slang to ultra-formal legal prose
- **Humor in Spanish** — double entendres, cultural references, irony markers (no te digo)
- **Rhetorical devices** — anaphora, chiasmus, litotes as used in Spanish oratory
- **Creative writing** — microcuentos, poetry composition, narrative voice exercises

### 8.3 Professional Mastery
- **Simultaneous interpretation drills** — shadow a Spanish audio while translating mentally
- **Academic writing** — thesis structure, literature review conventions, citation in Spanish (APA en español)
- **Conference presentation** — hedging, Q&A handling, persuasive academic rhetoric
- **Technical writing** — user manuals, scientific papers, patent descriptions

### 8.4 Cultural Fluency
- **Current events discussion** — reading El País, La Nación, El Universal with full comprehension
- **Political discourse** — understanding parliamentary debates, party ideologies across Hispanic countries
- **Generational slang** — Gen Z Spanish internet slang, meme culture, TikTok vocabulary
- **Taboo language and euphemisms** — understanding without necessarily using; cultural sensitivity
